Got Questions?

Frequently Asked

A .sol subdomain is a short, human-readable name that points to your Solana wallet address — for example, yourname.mytag.sol. Instead of sharing a 44-character wallet address, you share your name. It is registered through the Solana Name Service (SNS) and stored as an on-chain asset directly in your Solana wallet. Nobody can take it from you, move it, or modify it without your private key — it is yours in the same way any other on-chain asset is yours. ²

Yes — SNS subdomains are currently offered as one-time purchases with no annual renewal fees. Unlike traditional domain names (which you rent year after year), you pay once and own it as long as the Solana Name Service (SNS) protocol continues to operate. ²

Once registered, your subdomain resolves to your Solana wallet address. Anyone using a compatible wallet can simply type your .sol name in the send field instead of your long wallet address. No more copy-paste errors, no more double-checking every character, and no QR codes needed.

Prices for subdomains through the registrars listed here start from 3 USDC for names of 5+ characters, with higher tiers for shorter names — all shown transparently at sns.id before you confirm. On top of the registrar price, SNS charges a small platform fee plus a network transaction fee (gas). There are no recurring costs — you pay once and it is yours. ¹ ²

Your subdomain is an on-chain asset that you can transfer to any Solana wallet at any time — it's fully portable and yours to move freely. Note that selling or listing subdomains on marketplaces is not currently supported; only direct transfers are possible at this time.

Any wallet or app that integrates SNS resolution works — including Phantom, Backpack, Solflare, and Trust Wallet, as well as a growing list of DeFi protocols, exchanges, Solana dApps, and blockchain explorers. See the full list of integrations here. Use your .sol name throughout the Solana ecosystem. SNS is the leading name service on Solana and ecosystem support is expanding continuously.
